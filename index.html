This graphic shows that the prices with the highest frequency lie in the 25 dollars to 50 dollars range. This statistic is relevant to our observation question because we want to see the range of prices we are working with and where the bulk of the product prices lie. It will give us a good starting point to see which products have outlier prices and look at their ingredients.

Correlation Between Price and Rank: -0.01858430560499439

Now, we want to see the relationship between 2 variables in this dataset: price and rank. Surprisingly, this scatterplot shows us that the higher the price, the lower the rank of the product. The correlation between the 2 variables is -0.01858, though, so there is a very weak negative relationship between these two variables. Again, this would be more relevant to if there was documentation on what factors affect rank, however it seems that price plays a factor into "rank," with a product being ranked higher if it is cheaper for the consumer.
